AI Benefits Cost & Utilization Analyzer

Flagship prototype: Bronze data ingested, AI model development in progress

Executive Summary

Employee benefit programs represent one of the largest controllable expenses for insurers and employers. Organizations often struggle to understand cost drivers or optimize plan performance because data is fragmented across HR systems, finance databases, and third-party reports.

The AI Benefits Cost & Utilization Analyzer prototype demonstrates how Predictive and Computer Vision AI *could* bring structure and insight to this challenge. Currently, the local Data Lakehouse environment is installed and Bronze-level datasets have been ingested. Early profiling and experimentation can begin, while predictive and computer vision model development is still in the planning and configuration stage.

Overview

This prototype explores how insurers and large employers can analyze benefits data more effectively. By combining structured and unstructured data sources, it provides a clearer view of utilization patterns, emerging cost drivers, and potential areas for savings. It serves as a safe, local environment to experiment with AI-driven analytics before scaling to enterprise systems.

Key Features

  • Predictive AI: Forecast trends using historical claims, demographics, and seasonality (planned).
  • Computer Vision: Extract data from claim and invoice images (planned).
  • Conversational Analytics: Test natural-language queries on benefits data (planned).
  • Generative Reporting: Produce executive summaries with charts and insights (planned).
  • Governed Architecture: Demonstrates secure experimentation with AI before enterprise deployment.

Target Users

  • HR analytics and benefits teams
  • Insurance finance and pricing analysts
  • Data science teams supporting benefits management
  • Executives responsible for plan design and cost optimization

Business Value

  • Improve cost visibility and forecasting accuracy
  • Reduce manual data preparation and reporting time
  • Identify underutilized plans and cost-saving opportunities
  • Support data-driven decision-making for benefits design and risk management

Technology Overview

The prototype integrates open healthcare and economic datasets into a local Data Lakehouse environment built with MinIO, Apache Spark, Dremio, and Project Nessie. Bronze-level datasets have been ingested for profiling and early experimentation. Model development, Streamlit interface, and AI-driven analytics are still in configuration and planning stages.

Public datasets include CMS Inpatient Claims, Medicare Severity DRG classifications, BLS Employment and Benefits data, and regional GeoJSON boundaries. These are ingested into the Bronze layer for profiling, normalization, and early experimentation. Future phases will promote data to Silver and Gold layers to support predictive modeling, computer vision extraction, and executive analytics.

Current Status

Local Data Lakehouse environment is installed with MinIO, Apache Spark, Dremio, and Project Nessie. Bronze-level datasets have been ingested, allowing initial profiling. Configuration issues are being resolved, and coding for predictive and computer vision models is planned but not yet started.

MVP Scope (Planned)

  • Local prototype demonstrating cost and utilization forecasting
  • Claims image extraction using Computer Vision
  • Natural-language query testing through chatbot interface
  • Generative executive summary output

Roadmap (Future Enhancements)

  • Phase 2: Expand chatbot and add automated reporting features
  • Phase 3: Integrate with enterprise systems like Workday and SAP SuccessFactors
  • Phase 4: Extend predictive models to underwriting and claims automation
  • Phase 5: Develop enterprise dashboard for real-time analytics and governance metrics
