My Point of View on Enterprise AI

Most organizations are not failing at AI because of the models. They are struggling because they are applying AI to problems that have not been translated into clear, actionable decisions.

AI does not solve problems directly. It supports decisions that address those problems. If the decision is not clearly defined, owned, and measurable, AI introduces risk instead of value.

Core Insight

I view AI as a decision-support capability, not a decision-maker. The decision must be explicit, ownership must be assigned, and outcomes must be measurable and validated.

How I Approach It

  • Start with the decision: define what needs to be decided, who owns it, and what success looks like
  • Separate pattern work from judgment: AI supports pattern recognition, humans own tradeoffs
  • Keep AI in an advisory role with required human validation
  • Track false positives, false negatives, and business impact
  • Assign ownership and monitor outcomes through feedback loops

My Product Approach

Start with the Decision

I define the decision we are trying to improve, who owns it, and what action it drives. If the decision is unclear, I do not move forward.

Decompose the Workflow

I map the end-to-end workflow, separating data retrieval, rules-based logic, pattern recognition, and human judgment to identify where AI actually fits.

Choose the Right Approach

I determine whether the problem requires rules, search, retrieval-based approaches, or machine learning. Not every problem needs AI.

Design Human + AI Interaction

I define what the AI does, what the human does, and where validation occurs. AI supports decisions, but humans remain accountable.

Define Success & Risk

I establish business metrics and assess risk early, ensuring outputs are measurable, reversible, and appropriate for the level of impact.

Pilot, Measure, and Scale

I start with a narrow use case, measure both output quality and workflow impact, and scale only when value, trust, and control are proven.
